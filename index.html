Evidence

Recovered Profile

Arctic Frost Owl

Scientific Name: Bubo Arcticus

Name: Arctic Frost Owl

Status: Threatened because of climate change

Location: Northern Canadian Arctic Region

The Arctic Frost owl is a rare and newly discovered species in the northen canadian arctic region. Our group of researches has been lucky to encounter this bird in its normal habitat. This species has unique adaptations that allow it to survive in harsh conditions of the northern tundra biome. It's been hypothesized that this species is an evolutionary branch of the commonly found snowy owl.

According to our research group, this species is the apex predator in its habitat. It maintains its dominance in the tundra by very advance thermal regulation, advance hunting techniques and extreme energy sufficiency.

Environment

Tundra: Biome Environment

Summary

Temperatures: -34°C, very short and cold summers

Precipitation: very low and desert like

Terrain form: permanently frozen ground(permafrost), rocky soil

Challenges to survival: freezing cold, lack of food, no shelter(treeless)

The tundra is the coldest and the driest biome on Earth. It is mostly a treeless plain with a layer of permanetly frozen ground called "permafrost". It is primarily located in the hight latitudes like the arctic, and the high altitudes like the alps.

The tundra has a very low biodiversity due to its harsh conditions and the vegetation include mosses, sedges and small shrubs. The animals in the biome such as polar bears, arctic foxes, caribou, musk oxen, lemmings etc. are very well adapted to survive in this environment.

Adaptation Logs

Entries

Log 1

Observation: They are gigantic with a record breaking wingspan of upto 5 metres.

How: Their large wings help them to glide extra silently up in the arctic air and help them hunt bigger prey including seals and baby polar bears.

Conclusion: This allows them to be highly effective predators at the top of the food chain in the tundra.

Log 2

Observation: Very low heat loss relative to other arctic species

How: They got a multi layered feather structure, which traps air from escaping its body

Conclusion: This acts as a natural insulation system. And allows it to survive in extreme cold temperatures upto -40°C without excessive energy usage.

Log 3

Observation: They get no frostbites

How: Their have an special system where blood heat up the extremities

Conclusion: This prevents frostbites in areas like fingers and beak when touching the snow and ice

Log 4

Observation: Nearly impossible to spot in both summer and winter

How: They change colour of their feathers seasonally.

Conclusion: Hides itself from prey in the tundra ladcape without needing any trees

Left: Photos taken of the The Arctic Frost while its camouflaged. Right: Photo taken with a specialised heat cam

Log 5

Observation: even though they hunt in the day, they continue to be active at night unlike the their closest arctic relatives such as the snowy owl

How: They got massive eyes that are highly sensitive to light which helps them see in near total darkness

Conclusion: They can notice highly camouflaged prey even in the arctic polar nights.

Log 6

Observation: They can survive more than 2 months without any food or prey

How: They can control and reduce the amount of energy their body consumes

Conclusion: Prevents starving to death in harsh unpredictable tundra conditions.

Behaviour

Behaviour

  • They are solitary creatures
  • Hunts while flying silently (day and night)
  • Targets most species in tundra from small mammals to larger prey like baby polar bears
  • They hunt on a vast territory because of the small amount of prey present in the tundra
  • Not aggressive to bigger animals such as polar bears unless threatened
